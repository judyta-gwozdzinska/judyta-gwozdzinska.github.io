Billie Jean King

~ Tenis player who won for all women ~



Billie Jean King was a champion American tennis player winning a total of 39 grand slam titles in an illustrious career. She also played a key role in fighting for greater equality between men and women’s tennis.


A brief timeline of Billie Jean King's life:

  • Born on November 22, 1943 in Long Beach, California to parents Bill, a firefighter, and Betty, a homemaker.
  • Graduated Long Beach Polytechnic High School in 1961.
  • In 1961, won Wimbledon Doubles Title while partnering Karen Hantze.
  • Left Los Angeles State College now California State University in 1964 to focus on tennis.
  • Married Larry King on September 17, 1965 in Long Beach.
  • In 1966, after thirteen unsuccessful attempts to win a Grand Slam singles title from 1959 through 1965, King at the age of 22 finally won the first of her six singles titles at Wimbledon and the first of twelve Grand Slam singles titles overall, defeating Court in the semifinals 6–3, 6–3 and Maria Bueno in the final.
  • In 1971, began an intimate relationship with her secretary, Marilyn Barnett.
  • In 1973, the US Open became the first major tournament to offer equal prize money for men and women, after King's campaign for equal prize money in the men's and women's games.
  • In 1973, King defeated Bobby Riggs in an exhibition match (dubbed "the Battle of the Sexes"), winning $100,000.
  • In 1973, King became the first President of the women's players union – the Women's Tennis Association.
  • In 1974, with husband Larry King and Jim Jorgensen, founded womenSports magazine and started the Women's Sports Foundation.
  • In 1981, Billie Jean was publicly outed as a lesbian, and as a result, she lost all of her endorsement deals.
  • In 1987, elected to the International Tennis Hall of Fame.
  • The marriage ended in 1987 after Billie Jean fell in love with her doubles partner, Ilana Kloss.
  • On August 12, 2009, awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the United States’ highest civilian honor.
  • In 2014, founded the Billie Jean King Leadership Initiative, a non-profit dedicated to addressing the critical issues required to achieve diverse, inclusive leadership in the workforce.
  • In 2019, the Long Beach, California City Council honored Billie Jean, a native of Long Beach, by naming its new library the Billie Jean King Main Library.
  • Her tireless efforts to fight injustice and discrimination worldwide continue today.
